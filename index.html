A Journey to Remember

As I sit here reflecting on our journey from Grade 11 to 12, I'm overwhelmed by the memories that flood back each one a snapshot of moments that made this chapter of my life unforgettable. We weren't just classmates, we were a family, growing up together through laughter, nervousness, and victories.

I remember how it all began in Grade 11. We were a group full of energy, curiosity, and hope. Even though we had the same class, six new faces joined us in Grade 12 (two boys and four girls) and eight classmates left. Despite the changes, we stayed close, and the bond we built continued to grow.

One of my favorite memories was when we watched Elemental together. That movie hit us all hard most of us cried over the emotional scenes, not afraid to show how much we were touched. It was one of those rare times we were completely quiet, just feeling together.

And then, of course, came the laughter during class reports. Whenever someone was reporting and something funny happened, we couldn't hold it in. But when it was our turn, the nervousness would kick in hard. We'd glance at each other with those "please save me" looks, and somehow, we always managed to get through it.

Our Year End Party in Grade 11 was magical. From the best smile (especially hers), to dancing, gaming, and our gift exchange it was pure joy. And who could forget when we surprised Sir Flor on Teacher's Day with simple gifts and shared meals. I still miss that moment the excitement, the teamwork, and the smiles we all had.

Grade 12 was tougher but just as memorable. In FABM, we often found ourselves copying from each other when clueless about calculations. It wasn't laziness - we were leaning on each other, and that's what made it special.

Our dance practices were legendary - rehearsing under the blazing sun in Intramuros, even under a bridge in light rain. When we won 2nd place in the Intercultural Dance Competition, we screamed and jumped with joy. Sir Flor made it sweeter by surprising us with ice cream.

Not all moments were perfect. Our strict teacher would send people out for laughing. There were arguments too, but we always found our way back to friendship.

One memory I’ll never forget was on February 25, 2025, around 4:30–4:40 p.m when I finally confessed to her. I was nervous and didn’t know what to say, but I gave it my best. Afterward, my friends were excited, asking me what happened. I told them, word for word, what she said. That day still plays in my mind like a movie. A few weeks later, we made a short film for our MIL memory video, We walked around, laughed, and filmed scenes that I later edited. Watching that video now feels like a time capsule of everything we shared.

Our Work Immersion (Jan 20-Feb 3) was another adventure teachers cracking jokes while giving ABM career advice. Now at graduation, I'm excited yet heartbroken. I'll miss her smile, her voice all I have left is our graduation photo.

The laughter, the tears, the dances, the shared food they're all part of our story. We may go separate ways, but I hope we'll meet again someday, still carrying these memories in our hearts.

To the Class of 2025 Makanature, thank you for everything. I'll carry this with me forever.
